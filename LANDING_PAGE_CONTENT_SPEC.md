## What Changed (V1 → V2)

The product shifted from a **single-shot chord generator** (type a prompt, get 4 chords, download MIDI) to a **structured songwriting canvas** (create songs with multiple sections, generate/edit/refine chords per section, play back the full arrangement, export). The landing page needs to reflect this.

**V1 positioning:** "Conversational assistant for music theory"
**V2 positioning:** AI-powered songwriting workspace — prompt to full song structure

Key new capabilities to highlight:
- Multi-section song canvas (Verse, Chorus, Bridge, etc.)
- AI chord editing via natural language ("make it jazzier", "darker chorus")
- Interactive chord picker with harmonic function colors and guitar/piano diagrams
- Per-section and full-song playback with chord highlighting
- Key transposition, tempo controls, section repeats
- Full-song MIDI export (all sections, with repeats)
- Guided song creation flow (title → key → tempo → first section prompt)
- Smart prompt routing (same input box for generating and editing)

## Tone Guidelines

- **Confident but not hype-y.** Sonorus does real music theory — lean into that, don't oversell.
- **Musician-first language.** Say "chord progression" not "audio content". Say "verse and chorus" not "segments". Say "DAW" not "music software".
- **Accessible.** The tool is for everyone from beginners to working musicians. Don't gatekeep with jargon, but don't dumb it down either.
- **Short sentences.** The V1 site already does this well — keep it.
- **Show, don't tell.** Every claim should be backed by a screenshot, example prompt, or specific number.
